Farm, Sweet Farm （2）

Raising Animals

In your Coop and Barn, you can raise cows, chickens, and other animals purchased in Mineral Town! If you raise your livestock with love and care, they'll produce byproducts like Eggs and Milk. As your friendship grows, so too will the quality and value of their byproducts.

How to Raise Your Animals

Animals are like people: the quickest way to their hearts is through their stomachs! Leave Feed or Fodder in your livestock's Feeding Bins once a day, or give it to them directly to raise their affection. You can also talk to them and brush them to make them even happier!

Don't neglect to feed your animals! Babies can't grow if they don't eat, and unfed adults won't produce byproducts. Don't let them get hangry!

About Feed and Fodder

You can buy Feed and Fodder at shops around Mineral Town, but if you're feeling thrifty, you can make your own! Chicken/Rabbit Feed can be made by putting Corn into your Water Mill. To make your own Fodder, grow some Grass in your field, then cut it down.

Putting Animals Out to Pasture

You might notice a Bell by your Coop and Barn. If you ring it, your animals will go out to pasture. While they're outside, you don't have to worry about feeding them; animals will eat Grass and find their own food as long as they're outside long enough to get their fill!

Baby Animals!

If you give adult cows, alpacas, sheep, or Angora rabbits a Breeding Kit, they will become pregnant and eventually give birth. If you place an Egg from a chicken into the Incubator, it'll hatch into a chick! Baby animals need to eat to grow big and strong, so make sure you feed them every day.

*Alpacas, sheep, and Angora rabbits can only be given Breeding Kits when their coat is ready to shear.

Obtaining Byproducts

You can obtain byproducts from your adult animals. Chickens will leave their Eggs on the floor of their Coop every morning. Cows, sheep, alpacas, and Angora rabbits will need the following tools if you want to obtain byproducts from them:

Milker Using the Milker will allow you to milk cows.
Clippers Using the Clippers will allow you to shear sheep, alpacas, and Angora rabbits.

Caring for Pets

Livestock aren't the only animals you can own—your farm is the perfect home for a variety of pets! During Year 1, for instance, you'll be asked to care for a horse. Later, after fulfilling certain requirements, you'll also be able to purchase pets like cats and dogs from the Pet Shop, which comes to town once per season. Train your pets well, and you can enter them in festivals like the Fetch Fest and the Derby!

Raising a Pet

Unlike livestock, pets do not need to be given Feed or Fodder. But if you give them Pet Treats and speak with them, their affection for you will grow. Once you've purchased a Ball or Disc, you can train them too!

*Brush your horse every day to make it extra happy!

*The Ball and Disc can be bought from Huang.

*Treats are sold by Van at his traveling Pet Shop.

Horsin' Around

After 90 days, the foal on your farm will mature into an adult horse. If you've taken very good care of it, Mugi will let you keep it! Press the A Button near your horse to ride it, and press the B Button to dismount. If you're in a hurry, don't hoof it alone—take your trusty steed!
